# Impeccable — Design Context for Ovid

Ovid is a bilingual EPUB reader. Tap any paragraph to flip between the original and the translation. This document defines the aesthetic frame for every design decision in the project. Read it before touching type, color, layout, or motion.

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary reader: the heritage / diaspora reader.**
Someone who half-knows the language — it carries family, childhood, a grandparent's voice, a half-remembered rhythm — but they're stronger in the other one. They read mostly in their stronger language and peek at the original for feeling, cadence, a word that doesn't translate. The translation isn't a crutch; the original is an emotional anchor. They are returning to something, not studying it.

This reader is also: the language learner (secondary) and the literary explorer (secondary). Ovid serves them too, but the heritage reader's emotional temperature — quiet, personal, a little wistful — sets the tone.

**Contexts of use (all four, equally):**
- Phone in bed at night, dim room, one-handed
- Phone on the commute, short bursts
- Laptop at a desk, longer sit-down sessions
- Tablet on the couch, held like a book

Because Ovid lives on every surface and in every lighting condition, **light and dark themes are both first-class.** Neither is a courtesy mode.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: literary · patinaed · quiet.**

Concrete reference object: a well-loved Penguin Clothbound Classic pulled off a shelf after years — the cloth is softened at the corners, the gilt is slightly worn, the paper has warmed. Or: a used paperback you found in a secondhand shop with a previous owner's name in pencil on the inside cover.

**Emotional goals:** return, recognition, quiet pleasure, respect for the text. Never: achievement, novelty, urgency, performance.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Palette anchor:** cream paper, walnut, ink, with oxblood as a rare accent. Neutrals tint toward warm (the hue axis is roughly 40–60 OKLCH — paper, parchment without being parchment). Accent only appears where it must — a single mark of emphasis per screen, not a repeated motif.

- **Light theme:** warm cream (not #fff), ink-dark body text (not #000), walnut for secondary/metadata, oxblood reserved for one emphasis per view.
- **Dark theme:** deep warm ink (a walnut-black, never pure), cream-warm body text, muted accent. Do not use the AI cyan-on-dark / neon-on-black palette. The dark theme is a reading lamp at 11pm, not a terminal.

**Typography direction:** type IS the interface. Ovid is a reading app; the type carries the mood.
- Pair a display serif with real character — something with letterpress bite, a little aged, evocative of an old title page. Consider faces like Redaction, Bluu Next, Editor's Note, Parafina, Reckless Neue, Sainte Colombe. Avoid the reflex picks (Fraunces, Playfair, Cormorant, Crimson).
- Body: a warm bookish serif designed for long reading. Consider Vollkorn, EB Garamond, Spectral, Gentium Plus, Cardo, Source Serif 4. Avoid the reflex picks.
- CJK: the project already uses LXGW Neo ZhiSong Screen (song-ti, print-formal). For diaspora warmth, consider pairing with or offering LXGW WenKai (kai-ti, hand-written warmth) for display / titling. Source Han Serif also works.
- **Hard rule:** no Inter, no system-ui fallback as the display face, no monospace shorthand for "developer/technical."

**References (good):**
- Penguin Clothbound Classics and NYRB Classics — restrained, confident, literary
- Are.na and Readwise — quiet information architecture, serifs that respect the reader
- iA Writer — discipline about what's on screen
- A used bookshop's shelf card, not a Kindle store

**Anti-references (explicitly reject):**
- Generic SaaS dashboards — no rounded Inter, no mint-to-blue gradients, no stat cards, no "get started" illustrations
- Language-learning apps (Duolingo-ish) — no streaks, mascots, loud progress bars, gamification, candy colors
- Over-decorated vintage kitsch — no gold flourishes, no parchment-texture backgrounds, no fake leather, no wood-grain drop shadows. Patina comes from hue and type, not from texture assets.
- Cyberpunk dark readers — no neon on pure black, no glow borders, no monospace atmosphere

### Design Principles

Use these as tiebreakers. When two options are both plausible, pick the one that better serves these.

1. **Type is the room.** The interface dresses the text; it never competes with it. Layout and chrome recede; typography carries the mood. If a design decision makes the type work harder, choose it.

2. **Patina comes from hue and type, not texture.** Warmth is built into the color values, the letterforms, the spacing rhythm. Never from parchment JPEGs, wood-grain backgrounds, or drop-shadow trickery. A reader in 2045 should still find it quiet and confident, not dated.

3. **Invisible until summoned.** The translation is a bridge the reader walks across only when they want to. It should never announce itself. The original breathes first. Every toggle, progress indicator, and control fades back until called.

4. **Rare accent, weighted meaning.** Oxblood (or whatever the single accent is in context) appears once per view at most. Its power comes from scarcity. Do not repeat it for decoration, do not use it for every interactive element, do not fall back to it when stuck.

5. **Held, not used.** Interactions feel like turning a page, not clicking a control. Fades over swaps, gentle deceleration over bounce, slow over snappy when there's a choice. The reader is settling in, not rushing.

6. **Both lamps are equal.** Light and dark are both designed first-class. Neither is a mode; both are rooms. Test every surface in both. A component that only sings in one is not done.

7. **No AI tells.** Reject: gradient text, `background-clip: text`, colored side-stripe borders, card grids with icon-above-heading, sparkline decoration, modal-first flows, glassmorphism for decoration, generic drop-shadowed rounded rectangles. If the interface could be mistaken for AI output, redesign it.

### Project Constraints

- **Stack:** React 18 + CSS (no Tailwind, no CSS-in-JS). Edit existing `src/index.css`, `src/App.css`, and per-component CSS files in `src/components/`. Prefer modern CSS (oklch, color-mix, light-dark, container queries, @layer).
- **Existing metaphor to honor:** bookshelf with spines, AI-generated covers, paragraph-tap toggle, infinite scroll across chapters. The skeuomorphic shelf is emotionally correct for this product — refine it, don't throw it out. But strip any wood-grain / leather / parchment textures that drift toward kitsch.
- **Languages on screen:** Latin + CJK must coexist gracefully. Line-height and vertical rhythm must work for both. Test every reading surface with English and Chinese content.
- **Accessibility:** the reader is often in bed, tired, or on a small screen. Generous tap targets, readable contrast in both themes, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for the paragraph toggle and any transitions.
